Privacy Commissioner Concerns About Online Circulation of Video Clips and Photos of Private Activities in Hotel Rooms Emphasising the Use of Recording Devices and Disclosure of Data Should Avoid Intrusion into the Privacy of Individuals


(January 10, 2018) Concerned about the incident in Hong Kong recently, the privacy videos and photos circulated by hotel guests in the room were disseminated and aroused media attention and coverage. The Personal Data Privacy Commissioner ("Privacy Commissioner"), Hong Kong, , And expressed the following opinions on the incident:
  • The Privacy Commissioner emphasizes that private use of any device (including cameras, camcorders, aerial cameras, etc.) is deliberately collected in any place (such as hotel rooms, private residences, etc.) where privacy is a concern It also violates the Personal Data (Privacy) Ordinance ("Privacy") by identifying the identity of the individual by recording the identity of the individual, where and when they are located and uploading to the Internet or through other channels Ordinance ") is very risky.
  • The definition of "personal data" under the Privacy Ordinance refers to information that exists in the form of records and is directly or indirectly related to an identifiable person. And the collection of personal data , the "Privacy Policy" under the Data Protection 1 (2) in principle provides that personal data shall be lawful and in the circumstances of the case is a fair way to collect.
  • The method of collecting personal data is fair and depends on individual circumstances:
    • In the past, the Office of the Privacy Commissioner for Personal Data and the Administrative Appeals Board handled complaints from three media agencies about the candidacy of two media organizations for their activities at home. According to the decision of the Administrative Appeals Board, regardless of their social status and occupation, their privacy should be protected against unreasonable interference. However, the long-distance shooting of artists by artists in their dwellings, including the use of televisions and amplifiers, by the media, has gone beyond the reasonable expectation of individuals living in the home to privacy of personal data at home. It is not fair to collect the personal data of an artist in this way unless the filming is necessary for disclosure of matters involving "public interest".
    • The Privacy Commissioner reminds members of the public to be "self-protecting." They should always beware of any privacy violations even when they are in private areas. They should also respect the personal data privacy of others and refrain from uploading and disclosing others' intimate images and photos , So as not to violate the "Privacy Ordinance" or other criminal or civil liability.
